I'll be home for Christmas - Walter Kent & James "Kim" Gannon

Translated into ASL by David Bar-Tzur

Created 7 December 2005, links updated monthly with the help of LinkAlarm.

EXPLANATION OF THE GLOSSING SYSTEM
(to understand how I describe the signs in this translation).

ME VISIT HOME DURING CHRISTMAS.
YOU DEPEND-ON-me ME CAN.
PLEASE HAVE SNOW
HAVE BERRY HOLD-berry-OVER-head KISS-person.
NEED PRESENT UNDER TREE SET-IN-CIRCLE.
CHRISTMAS E-V-E ME VISIT.
ENJOY L-O-V-E SHINE-DOWN INSPIRED.
ME VISIT HOME DURING CHRISTMAS.
BUT MAYBE ONLY DREAM.
CHRISTMAS E-V-E ME VISIT.
ENJOY L-O-V-E SHINE-DOWN INSPIRED.
ME VISIT HOME DURING CHRISTMAS.
||: BUT MAYBE ONLY DREAM. :||

Notes: ME - Underlined prounouns (ME, YOU, HE) are honorific index (HON.-IX), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

PRESENT - (2h)[X], POs ><, FOs up, nod downwards at wrist to show that something is given as a gift.

BERRY HOLD-berry-OVER-head KISS-person - [O] twists FT against FT of NDH [1], then [F] dangles the mistletoe over the head of someone with the NDH and with the NDH left in place the DH touches [O^] to lips and then to the lips of the imaginary person in front of her/him.

SET-IN-CIRCLE - [A dot], PO > NDS, FO away, set the gifts in a circle under the tree.

SHINE-DOWN - [O^] is held high PO > signer; hands open to [5^] while moving > signer.

The signs between "||:" and ":||" are to be repeated.
