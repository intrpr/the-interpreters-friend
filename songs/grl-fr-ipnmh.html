Notes: (<) TALL, (>) TAN, (<) YOUNG, (>) SHAPELY - Sway to left and then to right with each adjective. SHAPELY is the hearing gesture where (2h)[B] trace the outline of torso, waist, hips.

THE - Underlined words are honorific indices ("indexes"), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

BRAZIL - [B], PO away, FO up, descends in a "Z" pattern.

BEACH - NDH [Bb], PO down, FO away, acts as the shore line, and the DH [5], PO down, FO > NDS, wg while moving towards and spilling over onto the dorsal side (backhand) of the NDH, receding and spilling over again, like waves.

TAKE-WALK - [F], PO down, FO away, nods downwards while moving away.

PASS-EACH-OTHER - (2h)[1], DH PO away, NDH PO > signer, FOs up, hands moves in the direction of the PO.

LOOK-BACK - [V], PO down, FO and eyes look > NDS then both look away as if the eyes are now looking > center where the girl is.

HAND-ON-HEART'ahhh' - put palm of [5] against heart as if taking in a deep breath while mouthin "ahhh!"

SAMBA - Stand in place but (2h)[5], POs away, FOs up, held high with head, torso, and hands swaying from side to side.

HIPS-SWAY - (2h)[B], POs ><, FOs away, with head and hands swaying from side to side.

CRAZY-FOR - [5:], PO > singer, FO up, is shaken slightly from side to side.

GIVE-HEART-TO - (2h)[C], POs ><, knuckles are curled so that hands make a valentine shape; this is held against heart and then brought forward.

STRAIGHT-AHEAD - Same as STRAIGHT.

LOOK-AT-me - [V], PO down, FO away, FTs turn around and "look" at signer's face.

ME++ - Poke chest with index finger several times.

The signs between "||:" and ":||" are to be repeated.

Lyrics:

Tall and tan and young and lovely,
The girl from Ipanema goes walking,
And when she passes each one she passes goes "a-a-ah!"
When she walks she's like a samba that,
Swings so cool and sways so gentle,
That when she passes each one she passes goes "a-a-ah!"

Oh, but I watch her so sadly,
How can I tell her I love her?
Yes, I would give my heart gladly.
But each day when she walks to the sea,
She looks straight ahead not at me

Tall and tan and young and lovely,
The girl from Ipanema goes walking,
And when she passes I smile, but she doesn't see,
She just doesn't see, No she doesn't see.
