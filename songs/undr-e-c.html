Notes: LOOK-FORWARD - Same as LOOK-UP-TO.

RIGHT-HERE - (2h)[1^], POs down, FOs away, hands descend 1x or 2x.

(2h)YOU - Underlined words are honorific indices ("indexes"), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

BELOW - [1^], PO down, FO away, descends in a curvy patternt to show "way down there".

SWEETIE - SWEET.

[SWIM] AROUND - (2h)[1], POs > signer, circle around each other while both hands move > DS.

(2h)#ALL - (2h)[A], POs ><, FOs up, are held at shoulder height and move away while opening to (2h)[L].

CRASH - NDH [B], PO > DS, FO up, acts as the rocks, and the DH [5], PO > NDS, FO up, crashes against it, wg as it moves up and recedes. Repeat.

FLIP-PAN - Mime holding a pan and flipping a fish that is cooking in it.

HAVE-NO - (2h)[B], POs > signer move upwards past mouth and swing upwards and apart while mouth blows on hands as they pass.

HOOK - Hook [X] into side of mouth.

BUBBLES - (2h)[S], POs up, FOs away, alt. move up like juggling, opening to [5:] and then closing again.

(newt) - It is imposible to capture all the puns and impractical to fingerspell the series of animals, so I used undersea animals that have signs and tried to pick a musical activity that would look humorous if that animal did it. Since this will make it harder to keep one's place, I have included key words in parentheses before the signed line to help.

SQUID - Sign looks like AND signed twice while moving > DS, but not closing completely.

PLAY-CLARINET - For these and other musical insturment, mime how they are played.

JELLYFISH - [5], PO down, FO away, moves up twice, curling and uncurling fingers.

CRAB - (2h)[V], POs ><, FOs away, closes twice to (2h)[U] with a scissors action.

V-PLAY-BASS - (2h)[V]: Imagine NDH high on the frets while DH strokes strings while mouthing "boam boam boam" (to rhyme with "roam").

COOL - FINEwg.

WHALE - (2h)[B], horizontal NDS arm represents the surface of an ocean. The movement of the DH shows a whale diving in and out of the water > NDS. This sign can be done with DH [W]. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - whale.

BLOW-HOLE - [S] touches dorsal (back) side of hand to top of head and opens to [5^] while rushing upwards and air is blown between lips.

LOBSTER - (2h)[V], POs down, FOs away, closes twice to (2h)[U] with a scissors action.

SHARK - (2h)[B], touch midpoints with DH vertical and behind horizontal NDH, both move forward in a wavy swimming pattern. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - shark.

TOOTHY-SMILE - (2h)[5:], POs >&l;t, FOs > NDS, with DH on top of NDH. The hands sweep in a semi-circle > DS as if showing the range of teeth.

OCTOPUS - NDH [O^] is placed on top of [5] which wiggles its tentacles. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - octopus.

DOLPHIN - [R dot], that is, a combination of [L] and [R], PO > signer, FO > NDS, hand moves in a wavy motion > NDS as if riding the waves.

GOOD-AT - [F], PO > signer, Fo > NDS, strikes chin.

MOUTH-CHATTER - [O^], PO away, FO up, opens and closes like the motion in MEETING while lips move like a chattering dolphin.

SEAL - (2h)[B], cross wrists and slap dorsal (back) sides together.

HORN - [C] honks a horn like seals play with their mouths.

HONK++ - [C] holds dorsal (back) side against mouth and moves head while squeezing the horns.

PENGUIN - (2h)[B], POs down, FO > respective sides, are held at waist and body waddles, imitating the walk of a penguin. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - penguin.

CONDUCT - (2h)[1] move like a conductors baton.

TUXEDO - (2h)[S] cross at wrists under chin and shoot out once to (2h)[H].

BAND - MUSIC GROUP.

CLAM - (2h)[B^], FO away, are held like a closed clam shell, then open twice.

CASTANETS - (2h)[ILY] tap middle and ring fingers against palms with NDH held high and DH held at waist.

SNAIL - [1] inches its way up the dorsal side of the forearm by curling and uncurling.

FLY - Flap arms.

JUMP-IN-ARC - [B], PO down, FO > NDS, jumps in an arc > NDS.

SUCK - [1^], pokes at open mouth.
