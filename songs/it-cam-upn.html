Notes: LONG-AGO

CLOSER+ - Like ARRIVE, but DH gets closer, then closer to the NDH.

HARP - (2h)[open 8], move in circles, moving away and coming back as if gliding across the strings of a harp.

(2h)#ALL - (2h)[A], POs ><, FOs up, are held at shoulder height and move away while opening to (2h)[L].

GRACE - [O^] or [S], held high and PO > Heaven, supinates and opens to [5] while being brought towards head. For an animated gif of this sign, see Animated dictionary of religious signs - Deaf Missions: Grace.

THEM - Underlined prounouns (ME, YOU, HE) are honorific indices ("indexes"), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

MORE-AND-MORE - The sign MORE is repeated with the NDH held in place.

WING - (2h)[B^] are held out like the final position of ANGEL and the FT are fluttered slightly.

CAUSE - (2h)[S] are held at the chest as if holding a rope and move forward while opening to (2h)[5]. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - cause.

WIND BLOW - At first (2h)[5], POs ><, FOs away, move in small figure-eights to show the wind and then in larger, faster figure-eights to show the strength of the wind. Both signs are not strictly necessary, but done here to follow the rhythm of the music. You will notice that a number of signs are repeated for the same reason.

SETTLE-DOWN-ON - (2h)[5], POs down, FOs away, descend.

FLY - (2h)[B^] are held out like the final position of ANGEL and the arms show the wings flapping.

ABOVE - Like COLLEGE but NDH is PO down.

HAVE-PITY-ON - (2h)[open 8], POs away, hands move in vertical circles normal (perpendicular) to the chest.

TALK-AROUND - Like GOSSIP, but opening and closing [O^].

UP-TO-NOW - (2h)[1^], POs down, index fingers travel up the chest on the DS until they end (2h)[1], POs > singer, FOs up.

SORROW - SAD.

SIN - (2h)[X] touch their respective sides of the mouth, descend to neutral space and circle antisymmetrically parallel to the chest. For an animated gif of this sign, see Animated dictionary of religious signs - Deaf Missions: Sin.

BURDEN-PUSH+ - (2h)[B^] touch DS shoulder with FT and push down twice; each time the shoulder dips down > DS.

BEND-DOWN+ - [S] is held up like a head and the "head" bends down twice, further and further.

WORK"over time" - The sign WORK has DH moves in vertical circles several times while mouthing "sta".

LOOK"look++" - (2h)[V] point FTs > angels while mouthing "look look look".

SIT-DOWN - (2h)[B], POs down, FOs away, hands descend slowly.

PUT-ASIDE - (2h)[B], POs > DS, with DH higher, palms are pressed against imaginary wall that is pushed to one side.

KNOW-THAT - Starts like KNOW but twists and descends, looking almost like DON'T-KNOW except the hand twists down at the end.

HURRY-ON - HURRY.

BIBLE - This sign varies according to denomination. The most frequent are JESUS BOOK, GOD BOOK and B-LAW.

YEARS-AND-YEARS - YEAR is repeated with effort.

GO-IN-CYCLE+ - (2h)[1], POs towards, FOs ><. FT touch, DH moves in a circle normal (perpendicular) to the chest until FT touch again. This sign is repeated.

TIME-PERIOD - [T], PO > NDS, FO up, travels in a vertical plane parallel to the NDH and makes contact with NDH [B], PO > DS, FO away. For a QuickTime file of this sign, see ASL Pro: Period - Time.

LOOK-FORWARD - (2h)[V] FTs point diagonally up and move slightly in that direction twice.

REALLY-WANT - WANT but shaken back and forth to show intensity.

#BACK-TO - [B], PO away, FO up, fingers curl, then staighten to a [K], while moving towards Heaven.

COPY-above - Dorsal (back) side of NDH points > Heaven.

Lyrics:

1. It came upon the midnight clear,
That glorious song of old,
From angels bending near the earth,
To touch their harps of gold;
"Peace on the earth, good will to men,
From Heaven's all gracious King."
The world in solemn stillness lay,
To hear the angels sing.

2. Still through the cloven skies they come
With peaceful wings unfurled,
And still their heavenly music floats
O'er all the weary world;
Above its sad and lowly plains,
They bend on hovering wing,
And ever over its Babel sounds
The blessèd angels sing.

3. Yet with the woes of sin and strife
The world has suffered long;
Beneath the angel strain have rolled
Two thousand years of wrong;
And man, at war with man, hears not
The love-song which they bring;
O hush the noise, ye men of strife
And hear the angels sing.

4. And ye, beneath life's crushing load,
Whose forms are bending low,
Who toil along the climbing way
With painful steps and slow,
Look now! for glad and golden hours
Come swiftly on the wing.
O rest beside the weary road,
And hear the angels sing!

5. For lo! the days are hastening on,
By prophet-bards foretold,
When with the ever circling years
Comes round the age of gold;
When peace shall over all the earth
Its ancient splendors fling,
And the whole world send back the song
Which now the angels sing.
