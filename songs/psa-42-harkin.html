Psalm 42 (As the deer longs) - Dana Harkin

Translated into ASL by David Bar-Tzur

Created 4 November 2005, links updated monthly with the help of LinkAlarm.

EXPLANATION OF THE GLOSSING SYSTEM
(to understand how I describe the signs in this translation).

1. KNOW-THAT DEER REALLY-WANT ANIMAL-DRINK WATER, PARALLEL MY SOUL WANT DRINK-IN YOU, GOD. MY SOUL THIRST FOR GOD HIMSELF LIVE. ME SEE YOUR FACE WHEN?

2. MY TEARS ME SWALLOW DAY-AND-NIGHT. SOME ASK-me, (role play with sneer) YOUR GOD WHERE? (end role play) INSIDE-SELF FEEL GONE, BUT ME LOOK-BACK TIME-PERIOD PRAISE YOU INSIDE TEMPLE.

3. ME MOURN, STRUGGLE INSIDE-SELF FOR-FOR? ME HOPE GOD HELP-me SHOULD. ME SING PRAISE GOD AGAIN WILL. HIMSELF MY HOPE, HIMSELF MY HOUSE.

Notes: KNOW-THAT - Starts like KNOW but twists and descends, looking almost like DON'T-KNOW except the hand twists down at the end.

REALLY-WANT - the sign WANT but shaken back and forth to show intensity.

ANIMAL-DRINK - [5^], PO down, FO away, hand moves > mouth and closes mutliple times.

PARALLEL - (2h)[1], POs down, FOs away, move forward. For a QuickTime file of this sign, see ASL Pro: Parallel.

DRINK-IN - (2h)[5^], PO away, FO up, hand moves > mouth and closes mutliple times.

Underlined prounouns (ME, YOU, HE) are honorific index (HON.-IX), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

INSIDE-SELF - The FT of [C] are held against the chest and stuff the FT of [O^] into the [C] twice.

LOOK-BACK - (2h)[H or V] are brought back over the shoulder, POs down, FOs back.

TIME-PERIOD - [T], PO > NDS, FO up, travels in a vertical plane parallel to the NDH and makes contact with NDH [B], PO > DS, FO away. For a QuickTime file of this sign, see ASL Pro: Period - Time.

Lyrics:

1. As the deer longs for flowing streams, so longs my soul for you, O God. My soul does thirst for the living God, when shall I come to see your face?

2. My tears have fed me day and night, while some have said, "Where is your God?" But I recall as my soul pours dry, the days of praise within your house.

3. Why do I mourn and toil within, when it is mine to hope in God? I shall again sing praise to him. He is my help; he is my house.
