EXPLANATION OF THE GLOSSING SYSTEM
(to understand how I describe the signs in this translation).

Refrain: JESUS, JESUS. EVERYTHING THAT LIVE
SHOULD KNEEL, AND BOW-DOWN-TO-lf THE-lf LORD.

1. THROUGH HIM-lf WE LIVE, WE WALK, SUCCEED EACH DAY.
THROUGH HIM-lf CHRIST, THROUGH HIM-lf KING.
JESUS HIMSELF-lf LORD. Refrain

2. NOT ARROGANT HIMSELF-lf GOD HIS-rt #OWN SON
HE-lf WILLING BECOME LOWLY, BECOME SLAVE.
JESUS HIMSELF-lf LORD. Refrain

3. HE-lf LIVE OBEY HEAVENLY-FATHER REALLY-WANT.
ACCEPT HIS-lf DEATH, E-V-E-N CRUCIFIXION.
JESUS HIMSELF-lf LORD. Refrain

Notes: SUMMON - DH [B] taps NDH [B], PO down, FO > signer, then moves > signer while closing to an [A], PO away from signer, FO up, and moving up.

BOW-DOWN-TO - (2h)[B], POs away, FOs up, are held like the final position of GIVE-UP, then bend these arms twice while bowing at waist once.

THE - Underlined words are honorific indices ("indexes"), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

#OWN - Fingerspelled with a twist.

LOWLY - (2h)[B^], POs ><, FOs up, move downwards arcing towards the signer. This is the opposite of the sign ADVANCED. For an animated gif of ADVANCED, see Animated dictionary of religious signs - Deaf Missions: Exalted.

HEAVENLY-FATHER - (2h)[A], FOs up, DH - held against forehead, NDH close to but lower, both hands open to (2h)[5] while moving upwards. HEAVEN may be added to this. For a QuickTime file of this sign, see ASL Pro: Heavenly Father - 1.

REALLY-WANT - WANT but shaken back and forth to show intensity.

CRUCIFIXION - Pinky side of [S] is "driven" into palm of [B], PO away, FO up, then vice versa. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - crucify.

Lyrics:

Refrain: Jesus, Jesus, let all creation
bend the knee to the Lord.

1. In him we live, we move, and have our being;
in him the Christ, in him the king! Jesus, the Lord.

2. Though Son, he did not cling to godliness;
but emptied himself, became a slave! Jesus, the Lord.

3. He lived obediently his Father's will
accepting his death, death on a tree! Jesus, the Lord.
