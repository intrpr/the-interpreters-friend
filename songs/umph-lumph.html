Notes:

OOMPA LOOMPA DOOMPADEE DOO (and its varients: DEE and DA) - (2h)[O] touch FTs to opposite shoulders, (2h)[L] touch hips, (2h)[D] touch opposite temples, then touch same side temple.

BIG-QUESTION - like the sign QUESTION-MARK but both hands and all fingers (not the thumb) form a symmetric shape that is a question mark in the right hand and a reversed question mark in the left hand.

GUZZLE-DOWN - NDH [C] is held high like a container that is being poured into the mouth and DH [5] wg as it shows the container being emptied out.

DON'T - (2h)[B], POs down, cross at wrists, then uncross moving horizontally and emphatically. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - don't.

EAT-WITH-TRUNK - Move [O^] as if it were a trunk bringing food to the mouth. Be sure to exaggerate.

WHAT-WRONG - WRONG+.

DIABETES - SWEET SICK.

HEART-ATTACK - [open 8] touches heart, then fist hits NDH palm.

TRUE-BIZ - TRUE + (2h)[S], POs down, FOs away, DH brushes from side to side across NDH wrist.

(2h)JUST-FINE - (2h)[5], POs ><, FOs up, touch chest with thumbs then move away in an upward arc.

(Repeat DOOMPADEE DOO with singer, but lower and smaller as voice fades away.) - As the oompa-loompa's keep repeating their name, they get softer and softer. As a parallel, sign smaller and closer to the throat.

RUDE - ROUGH, that is, [5], PO > face, FO i[, twists and moves away, scraping FT against palm of [B] as they pass.

MANNERS - FANCY, that is, [5], PO > NDS, FO up, moves CW as seen from DS, brushing against chest as it passes.

#NO-GOOD - (2h)[N], POs donw, FOs up, are held next to one another, than twist, ending in (2h)[L], POs up, FOs away.

MISCHIEVOUS - (2h)[3], POs ><, FOs away, are held at neck level and move twice > signer while curling to [3:].

PAMPER - [B] strokes at DS of pate (top of head) as if petting a cat.

SPOIL - [B] strokes NDH [B] as if petting a cat.

CURSE - [5:], PO > singer, - held at mouth and moves > what - cursed while closing to [S], then fist is shaken at what is cursed.

SPECK - Hand is held PO up in somewhat of a [W] while the thumb is flicked against the little finger.
