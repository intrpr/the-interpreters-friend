EXPLANATION OF THE GLOSSING SYSTEM
(to understand how I describe the signs in this translation).

SUPPOSE ME SEE ONE-MORE SPRING, #DO-DO?
ME WATCH-rt ALL-DAY BIRD BIRD~SING.
THEY-rt BUILD N-E-S-T BEAK-POKES-NEST. TWO-OF-THEM@rt FLY-OFF SING.
NOT COPY BIRD NOT-YET FIND MATE.

ME WATCH-lf ANIMAL SCADS-OF.
MOTHER LAMB WITH BABY WHITE LIKE SNOW.
(<) HAIL-FALL, (>) WIND STRONG,
ME SEARCH FIND MUSIC (2h)THERE-ctr.

SUPPOSE ME SEE ONE-MORE SPRING, #DO-DO?
ME LEARN-LESSON LIFE UP-TO-NOW.
BEFORE ME TEND POSTPONE.
SUPPOSE ME SEE ONE-MORE SPRING, #DO-DO?
GO-AHEAD BELLY-LAUGH. TODAY SHORT.

FROM-NOW-ON NEVER WAIT FOR THINGS. ME GET-BENEFIT
BEFORE CHANCE GONE. GO-AHEAD SING HAPPY NOW++.

Notes: ONE-MORE - (2h)[O^], POs ><, FO s away, hold FTs against each other and the index finger of DH points straight up and then goes back to its oringinal position 2x.

BIRD~SING - Sign BIRD and then with hand still kept in place sway head from side to side while opening and closing "beak".

BEAK-PECKS-NEST - [bO] is held at nose like a beak and then pecks at [C], PO > DS, FO away.

FLY-OFF - (2h)[B] cross at wrists and hands flap like wings while showing the path of the flight.

HAIL-FALL - I-C-E + (2h)F-CL alt. fall to earth in random places.

(2h)THERE-ctr - Both upturned palms whose FT are > center descend slightly.

LEARN-LESSON - FT of [O^] touch forhead, and then hand opens to [B] and touches forehead with the palm.

BELLY-LAUGH - (2h)[5:], POs ><, FOs away, DH is above NDH and they move alt. to and fro like a vibrating belly.

FROM-NOW-ON - (2h)[B^], POs ><, FOs away, DHFT touches knuckle side of NDHFT and moves away in a downward arc. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - from now on.

GET-BENEFIT - Sometimes called TAKE-ADVANTAGE, but not the sign that can imply negativity. Here it means [5:], PO > signer, FOs > NDS, is laid at the end of the NDH fingers and moves along the palm > the signer, ending by closing to an [S] and moving upwards.

GO-AHEAD - (2h)[B^ dot], POs ><, FOs away, move forward in a jerk.

Lyrics:

If I might see another Spring
I'd listen to the daylight birds
That build their nests and pair and sing.
Nor wait for mateless nightingale;

I'd listen to the lusty herds,
The ewes with lambs as white as snow,
I'd find out music in the hail
And all the winds that blow.

If I might see another Spring
Oh stinging comment on my past
That all my past results in "if"
If I might see another Spring
I'd laugh today, today is brief;

I would not wait for anything that cannot last,
Be glad today and sing, and sing.
