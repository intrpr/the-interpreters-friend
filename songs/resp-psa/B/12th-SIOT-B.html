Notes: GO-AHEAD - (2h)[B^ dot], POs ><, FOs away, move forward in a jerk.

HE - Underlined words are honorific indices ("indexes"), using the FT of the upturned palm to indicate who is being honored.

ALLELUIA! - CLAP-HANDS~CELEBRATE + open hands into (2h)[5] while lifting.

SAIL - DH [B^], PO > signer, FO up, rests on dorsal (back) side of hand which is held as in TABLE. The signer blows into the DH palm and the "ship" moves forward.

CHURN-VIOLENTLY - (2h)[5], POs down, FOs away, show the violent motion of the water's surface.

AS-HIGH-AS - MAXIMUM, that is, DH [B], PO down, rises until it slaps bottom of NDH, PO down. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - maximum.

SAILOR - (2h)[B^] both close to (2h)[O^] while contacting NDS hips, then repeat the action for DS hips. For a QuickTime movie of this sign, see ASL browser - navy.

HEART-FLUTTER - (2h)[B], POs > signer, FOs ><, NDH is held away from body and DH flutters between body and NDH, slapping chest and NDH palm.

CALM-DOWN - (2h)[5], POs down, FOs away, descend slightly, stop and then descend further.

GENTLE-BREEZE - (2h)[5], POs ><, FOs away, gently sway from side to side.

CALM - (2h)[5], POs down, FOs away, descend slightly at NDS then at DS.

(2h)#ALL - (2h)[A], POs ><, FOs up, are held at shoulder height and move away while opening to (2h)[L].

Lyrics (Psalm 107:23-24, 25-26, 28-29, 30-31):

Response: Give thanks to the Lord, his love is everlasting. (or) Alleluia.

They who sailed the sea in ships,
trading on the deep waters,
These saw the works of the LORD
and his wonders in the abyss. Response

His command raised up a storm wind
which tossed its waves on high.
They mounted up to heaven; they sank to the depths;
their hearts melted away in their plight. Response

They cried to the LORD in their distress;
from their straits he rescued them,
He hushed the storm to a gentle breeze,
and the billows of the sea were stilled. Response

They rejoiced that they were calmed,
and he brought them to their desired haven.
Let them give thanks to the LORD for his kindness
and his wondrous deeds to the children of men. Response
