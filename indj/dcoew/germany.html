Beethoven - Days in a life. Film (1976) Germany, 110 min, Biography. Beethoven lived from 1770 to 1827. This film looks at the time from when he first started to become deaf in 1813. Within 8 years he was completely deaf.

Habla, mudita (1. Speak, little mute girl 2. Don Ramiro.) Film (1973) Spain/Germany, 85 min, drama. A climber is lost and given shelter by a shepherdess who is speaking impaired but capable of speech. She has been kept that way by her family. Her brother is genuinely deaf and a little crazy. The climber teaches her how to speak which is resented by her family and he is accused of rape and imprisoned. This is a remake of a 1947 film (not listed).

Das Haus im Ginster. Film (1992) Germany, 93 min, drama. A university professor on his way to meet his wife for a holiday in the South of France picks up his stepdaughter. On the long drive she never speaks and continues to be speaking impaired on arrival. While they wait for his wife she starts to seduce him. Catya tells me that the daughter is not deaf or speaking impaired.

Jenseits der Stille (Beyond silence.) Film (1996) Germany 109 min, Drama. In German and German sign language. A woman, Lara, from [childhood] has had to act as a go-between for her parents who are both deaf and speaking impaired... As a child she is committed to her parents and misses out on her schooling through putting their needs first. Growing up she develops an interest in music and the clarinet The story then jumps to 10 years ahead when Lara is eighteen. She is good enough on the clarinet to be considering studying at a conservatory. Her father is opposed but her mother persuades him to let her spend the summer with her aunt in Berlin where she can prepare for the entrance exam...

Mute witness. Film (1995) Germany / UK / Russian, 90 min, thriller. A mix of Russian and English dialogue. A speaking impaired special effects artist is witness to a filmed murder (a snuff movie)... Best treated as a farce... The attitude to Billy's deafness while exploitative does show some understanding which is really lost in a film as dire as this one.

Wrong move. Film (1975) Germany, 103 min, road movie? A variation on the road movie in which the protagonist tramps through Germany trying to understand himself and his country. Kinski plays a deaf, speaking impaired woman. Very loosely based on the novel Wilhelm Meister's apprenticeship by Johann Wolfgang von Goethe.

Die romantische Gallerie von Claudia Krämer. Claudia has found her own style in painting: the Romantic Art style! Her paintings show mythic themes and express warmth and love. Dieter Fricke, Künstler. For several years I have tried through my art to call attention to the situation of us Deaf and Hearing Impaired people and to fight for the recognition of Sign Language. I do not know how extensively Hearing people are aware of the extent to which we are invisible and isolated from society. German Sign Language is not fully recognized, and it must be stated that only a small percentage of Deaf and hearing impaired people are satisfied communicating with Hearing people through spoken language alone.

Manfred Mertz. Nature is shown as an element of the ocean and the One with the Cosmos and much else. Reiner Mertz lives like most hearing-impaired people in two worlds: in a world of silence and a world of sound that needs no explanation to "normal" people. For Hearing people, acoustical signs from the environment, such as voices, music, and noise are enormously important. Compared to hearing-impaired who can only capture impressions from such things as pictures, colors, mimic, and gestures. They are closed off from the acoustical world. Such an above-average picture-rich world comes into being for hearing-impaired as expressed in pictures. These serve as a principal motif for Reiner in his artwork.
