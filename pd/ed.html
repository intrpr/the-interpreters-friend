Education

Formal educational programs can serve us well in providing a structure and thorough grounding in various fields, even providing options for those who wish to be involved in activities beyond interpreting: teaching interpreters, teaching Deaf people, working for a Deaf or interpreter organization, providing workshops or assessment, or simply working outside of Deafness altogether someday. If you know of others, please email me the website at dbartzur@earthlink.net, and I will include it below. I only list the institutions that have some web information on their programs. See NAD's How do I become a Sign Language interpreter? and for the arguments for and against attending an Interpreter Education Program at all, see Interpreter Training Programs: Two sides of the coin.
